OpenAI strikes deal with Pentagon, hours after rival Anthropic was blacklisted
by Trump OpenAI agreed to Pentagon terms banning domestic mass surveillance and
autonomous weapons, with technical safeguards on classified networks, after
Anthropic's refusal to comply.

Bias Comparison Late on Friday, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman announced an agreement to
deploy OpenAI's AI models on the Pentagon's classified network, hours after
President Donald Trump ordered agencies to stop using Anthropic tools. After
weeks of tense negotiations, Anthropic refused Pentagon demands to remove
safeguards barring domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons, and Pete
Hegseth, U.S. Defense Secretary, designated Anthropic a supply‑chain risk. Sam
Altman noted OpenAI will deploy engineers and technical safeguards, and the DoD
agreed to safety principles banning domestic mass surveillance and requiring
human responsibility for use of force. Anthropic vowed to challenge the
Pentagon's designation in court and said it will seek legal recourse, with a
$200 million contract and a six‑month transition window for federal agencies.
This development signals broader national‑security implications for AI as U.S.
federal procurement authorities apply supply‑chain restrictions to a U.S. AI
developer, a shift previously used under Section 889 of the 2019 National
Defense Authorization Act for foreign telecoms.
